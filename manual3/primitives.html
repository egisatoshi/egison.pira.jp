Primitive Functions

We give the list of primitive functions, here. It consists of numeric functions,

Numeric Functions

  • +

    • It takes two integers as the arguments, and returns the sum of them.

  • -

    • It takes two integers as the arguments, and subtracts the second argument from the first argument.

  • *

    • It takes two integers as the arguments, and returns the product of them.

  • modulo

    • It takes two integers as the arguments.
    The result is the remainder from division of the first argument by the second argument.
    The sign of the result is equal to that of the second argument.

  • quotient

    • It takes two integers as the arguments.
    The result is the quotient from division of the first argument by the second argument.
    Let n, m be integers, and q, r be the results of (quotient n m), (remainder n m), respectively.
    Then, n = q * m + r always holds.

  • remainder

    • It takes two integers as the arguments.
    The result is the remainder from division of the first argument by the second argument.
    The sign of the result is equal to that of the first argument.
